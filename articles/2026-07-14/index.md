# Christianity uniquely holds together truths that people often separate


- For you are both fearfully powerful and yet intimately loving
- You are the highest Lord/King, and yet you are the lowest of servants
- We call him Shepherd, and yet he's a lamb
- The Creator was born.
- The Author of life died.
- The Judge stood before human judges.
- The Infinite entered the finite.
- the immortal who became mortal
- He is where infinite justice meets infinite grace
- Strength Through Weakness, by conquering death by dying
- Your are a king who who died for his subjects
- You are the Judge that stands trial
- You are the judge the bears judgement
- victory through defeat
- life through death
- finding life through losing it
- At the cross, infinite grace meets infinite justice - perfectly
- At Calvary, infinite justice is satisfied, and infinite grace is freely given.
- Justice is not abandoned for grace; at the cross, infinite grace meets infinite justice



- for you bind:
  - Justice or mercy
  - Power or humility
  - Majesty or intimacy



- The cross is:
  - where infinite love meets infinite holiness
  - where perfect grace meets perfect justice
  - where the Lion triumphs as the Lamb
  - and where death itself becomes the doorway to life



### One of the deepest questions in Christian theology: Why do paradoxes exist in the Truths of God

Answer: the paradoxes exist because God is greater than the categories our finite minds are limited to create

### God's kingdom reverses human expectations

Jesus consistently taught that God's kingdom operates differently from worldly assumptions:

- Whoever exalts himself will be humbled.
- Whoever humbles himself will be exalted.
- The first shall be last.
- The greatest is the servant.
- Lose your life to find it.
