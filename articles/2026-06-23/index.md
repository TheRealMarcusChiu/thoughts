Beauty? Whether you find it in a quiet walk along the countryside, in someone you love, or in a "ratatouille dish" that transports you back to the comforts of childhood, they all share one thing in common—or perhaps two. This is beauty.

  

I remember lying in bed late one night, fresh from an all-day hangout. I was feeling a deep sense of gratitude. I didn’t know those people well, yet I genuinely enjoyed their company, especially how to interacted with each other as I sat on the side observing them like an audience member. I found myself fascinated by humanity's capacity to form such bonds - a group gathering with no goal other than to enjoy and be enjoyed by each other's presence. The following Sunday, I slept in, put on a worship playlist, and just laid in bed. It struck me then that beauty possesses two specific properties. First, it is inexhaustible; you never grow tired of it. No matter how many times you experience it - be it a hundred or ten thousand - each moment feels as fresh as the first. Second, despite its permanence, every encounter is deeply satisfying. It satiates. Every bite feels like a feast at a king’s table (or insert your favorite restaurant). Whether it is a fleeting glance or a small moment, it is always enough.

  

I remember arriving early at a newly opened boba shop, waiting in the parking lot for a friend. I saw her car pull up from a distance. It was the last time I would see her before she moved to a different city. As she stepped out of her car and walked toward me, our eyes locked, and I couldn't help but smile ear-to-ear. Seeing her felt like seeing her for the very first time. "She is so beautiful," I said to myself. But this time was different than the first, because I had come to know her as a person. She had this impeccable nature that made me want to know her - I can almost see her smile even now as I write this. That smile was the essence of who she was. If beauty has a magnitude, then the third thing is that true beauty is not merely inexhaustible and satiating; but it also grows in size and depth with every interaction. The second time is greater than the first. And so is the third, the fourth, and the fifth. Beauty never ends.
