# Excellencies of Christ

The Jonathan Edward's sermon `Excellencies of Christ` is built around **Revelation 5:5–6**:

> "Behold, the Lion of the tribe of Judah..."
>
> "...and I saw a Lamb, standing, as though it had been slain."

Edwards argues that Christ possesses **"an admirable conjunction of diverse excellencies"**—that is, qualities that we normally think of as opposites are perfectly united in Him.

## The "admirable conjunction" of Christ's excellencies

Edwards highlights pairings such as:

* **Infinite majesty and profound humility**
  * He is the King of kings.
  * Yet He was born in a manger and washed His disciples' feet.
  - The throne reached down to the manger.
  - The King knelt before His subjects.
* **Infinite justice and infinite grace**
  * He perfectly judges sin.
  * He freely forgives sinners.
* **Absolute sovereignty and complete submission**
  * He rules over all creation.
  * Yet He submits to the Father's will, even unto death.
* **Lion-like power and lamb-like meekness**
  * He conquers Satan, sin, and death.
  * He offers Himself as a sacrifice.
  - He broke the chains of death with nail-pierced hands.
  - The Almighty chose meekness without surrendering power.
* **Supreme glory and deepest suffering**
  * He is worshiped by angels.
  * He endures the shame of the cross.
  - The crown of glory passed through a crown of thorns.
  - His deepest humiliation unveiled His highest glory.
  - He shone brightest upon the darkest hill.
* **Perfect holiness and boundless mercy**
  * He cannot tolerate evil.
  * He welcomes repentant sinners.
  - His holiness exposes my sin; His mercy covers it
* **Infinite transcendence and intimate nearness**
  * He upholds the universe.
  * He dwells among His people.
  - Infinity learned to cry in a cradle.
  - The Word without beginning entered time

## Why Edwards thought this mattered

Edwards believed Christ's beauty is found not merely in each excellence individually, but in **their perfect union**.

A lion without the lamb might inspire fear.

A lamb without the lion might seem powerless.

But Christ is both:

* strong enough to defeat evil,
* gentle enough to save the weak.

Likewise:

* justice without grace would leave us condemned,
* grace without justice would leave evil unaddressed.

In Christ, both are fully expressed.

## Connection to your earlier question

What you called **"the great oxymorons of Christ"** is very close to Edwards' idea. He wouldn't call them contradictions, but rather **harmonious excellencies**—qualities that appear opposite from a human perspective yet are perfectly united in Jesus.

For example:

| Human perspective         | Christ's excellency                   |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Lion ↔ Lamb               | Majestic King and sacrificial Savior  |
| Grace ↔ Justice           | Forgives sin while satisfying justice |
| Sovereignty ↔ Servanthood | Rules all yet washes feet             |
| Strength ↔ Weakness       | Conquers through the cross            |
| Glory ↔ Humility          | Worshiped in heaven, born in a manger |
| Holiness ↔ Mercy          | Hates sin, loves sinners              |
| Eternity ↔ Incarnation    | The eternal Word became flesh         |

This theme has influenced many later Christian writers, including John Piper, who often points to Edwards' sermon as one of the clearest descriptions of the beauty of Christ. Piper summarizes the idea by saying that Christ's glory shines most brightly because all of His seemingly opposite perfections meet without conflict in one person.

In that sense, **"The Excellencies of Christ"** is essentially a meditation on why Jesus is uniquely beautiful: every virtue reaches its fullest expression in Him, and no excellence diminishes another. Instead, each one magnifies the rest.
